Weft · Rendered reference for builders

A living record of every component.

Weft is the design system Heddle uses. Every token, primitive, and pattern — in one place, at real sizes, with the code that renders them. Tokens are namespaced --weft-*; if you see a token without that prefix in a Heddle file, it's pre-Weft and needs to migrate.

Test for new components: toggle the page. If anything breaks, the fix is in the token layer, not the component. A component reaching for a raw hex, #fff, or rgba() outside the token block is the bug — route it through a token.

Persistence: theme resets on reload by design. v0 is deliberately stateless while the palette settles.

05 / Don’tsthe guardrails

What not to do.

×

Don’t introduce a third accent color. Blue and yellow are the full set. Functional states (ok / warn / stop) are tuned down, not saturated, and are not third accents.

×

Don’t use gradients on surfaces. The one exception is the yellow highlight-mark (linear-gradient yellow → transparent at 38–40%).

×

Don’t use pure black. Ink is #0b1020 — near-black navy.

×

Don’t use pure white for page backgrounds. Cream is the page. Paper (#ffffff) is for elevated card surfaces and contrast bands only.

×

Don’t bold the display serif past 500. Headlines live in the 300–500 range — high contrast comes from the typeface, not the weight. The mark and wordmark are SVG, not typeset, so the serif has no role in the logo.

×

Don’t place blue and yellow at equal weight on the same element. On a blue slab, yellow does the italic accents. On cream, blue does the italic accents and yellow does the highlight. Text on yellow is blue-ink.

×

Don’t stack more than two accent patterns in one headline. One italic-blue + one yellow-underline is the maximum.

×

Don’t put body text on yellow or yellow-soft. Yellow is for highlights, badges, and accents. Labels and prose go elsewhere.

×

Don’t animate sections below the fold on page load. Stagger-fade is hero-only. Everything else stays still until interacted with.
